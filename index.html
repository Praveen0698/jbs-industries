Balcony Doors

Balcony doors, often referred to as "patio doors" or "sliding glass doors," are a type of door commonly used to access a balcony or outdoor space from the interior of a building, typically a home or apartment. These doors are designed to provide both access to the outside and a view of the outdoor area while allowing natural light to enter the interior.

Balcony doors can be made from various materials, including wood, aluminum, steel, or uPVC, with glass panels to allow light to filter through. They often come with weatherstripping and insulation to enhance energy efficiency and weather resistance.

The choice of balcony doors depends on factors such as your architectural style, space available, climate, and personal preferences. Additionally, security features and locking mechanisms should be considered, especially for ground-level balconies, to ensure the safety of your home.

Lift And Side Doors

Lift Doors: Lift doors are doors used in elevators. There are automatic lift doors, which open and close automatically, and manual lift doors, which passengers need to open and close themselves.

Side Doors: Side doors are a general term used for doors located on the sides of various structures. In buildings, they can be exterior doors providing access from the side. In vehicles, such as cars or buses, they are the doors on the sides for passengers or cargo. In industrial settings, side doors offer access to loading docks or other specific areas. The design and function of side doors can vary widely depending on their context and purpose.

Tilt And Turn Windows

Tilt and turn windows are a versatile and innovative type of window commonly used in modern construction. These windows offer a unique combination of functionalities that make them suitable for a variety of applications.

The term "tilt and turn" refers to the two primary operations of these windows. First, they can tilt inwards at the top, allowing for secure ventilation without fully opening the window. This tilt function is especially useful for maintaining ventilation while keeping the window locked in a partially open position. It is a practical solution for rainy days or when you want to control airflow without compromising security.

The versatile design of tilt and turn windows makes them suitable for residential and commercial buildings. They are particularly popular in regions with strict building codes and energy efficiency requirements. With their dual functionality, they offer convenience, security, and practicality while contributing to the overall aesthetics and functionality of a building's design.
